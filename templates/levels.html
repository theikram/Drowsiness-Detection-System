Levels of Drowsiness

  • Alert:

    The user is fully awake and attentive.

    Normal blinking patterns with the eye aspect ratio (EAR) above the threshold.

    No signs of drowsiness detected.

    No yawning observed.

  • Slightly Drowsy:

    The user shows occasional signs of reduced alertness.

    EAR occasionally drops below the threshold, but not consistently.

    Minor blinks may be detected, indicating the user is starting to feel tired.

    Infrequent yawning may occur, suggesting the onset of drowsiness.

  • Moderately Drowsy:

    The user is showing clear signs of drowsiness.

    EAR frequently drops below the threshold.

    Multiple blinks and other indicators of drowsiness are detected.

    Yawning becomes more frequent, signaling increased drowsiness.

    The user should consider taking a break or resting.

  • Severely Drowsy:

    The user is at a high risk of falling asleep.

    EAR consistently remains below the threshold for a significant number of consecutive frames.

    Clear signs of drowsiness such as prolonged blinks, reduced responsiveness, and frequent yawning.

    Immediate action is required to ensure safety.

  • Asleep:

    The user appears to have fallen asleep.

    EAR remains below the threshold for an extended period.

    No responsive eye movements detected.

    Continuous yawning may still occur but without any response.

    Immediate intervention is necessary.
